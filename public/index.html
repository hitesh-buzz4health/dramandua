HIP

The hip joint is a ball and socket joint which links the pelvis with the upper end of the thigh bone. The ball is on the upper end of the thigh bone and this fits into a socket which is on the outer part of the pelvis. Both surfaces of the joint (the ball and the socket) are covered with white, smooth, low friction, glistening hyaline cartilage. This cartilage covering is akin to the tread on a tyre of a car. It can wear with excessive use or other provocative factors and unfortunately, cannot repair itself.
WHAT IS ARTHRITIS?
Arthritis is the term which refers to inflammation in and around the joint. In particular, it refers to the degenerative destruction of a joint with loss of the smooth glistening surface on both the ball and the socket. As the surfaces are lost, what was previously a low friction painless joint becomes a higher friction painful joint.
